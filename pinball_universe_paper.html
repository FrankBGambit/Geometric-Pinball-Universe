ABSTRACT

We present the Pinball Universe model, in which cosmic voids are not regions of empty space but active heat-pressure centres — the evacuated interiors of sequential Big Bang events in a cascading chain reaction. The central void, Boötes, acts as the origin of a φ-spaced hierarchy of satellite voids, each separated by the golden ratio. The scalloped boundary shape of Boötes is shown to be the geometric shadow of surrounding satellite bubble packing, with scallop count equal to satellite count — a testable prediction against real survey data.

We identify a systematic error in standard cosmological measurements: void sizes have been calculated using a converging mass-lens correction, when the correct physics requires a diverging heat-lens correction of opposite sign. This error causes all void radii to be underestimated by 15 to 40 percent, scaling as the void radius to the power of 0.55. Applying the correct thermal lensing correction shifts the Hubble constant from 67.4 to approximately 72 to 73 kilometres per second per megaparsec, resolving the Hubble tension without new physics. The CMB cold spot is identified as the divergent thermal lens signature of Boötes. Isotropy is shown to be a geometric consequence of golden-angle spiral packing from Boötes, not a postulate.

The Pinball Universe model proposes that the Big Bang is not an exception. It is the same pattern, operating at the largest scale with the longest timescales. The universe does not expand from a single point. It cascades — a chain of sequential bang events, each one triggered by the compression wave of its predecessor, each one carving a void in space and seeding the filament network where galaxies form.

The scalloped boundary of the Boötes void is the fossil of the squeeze. The neighbouring satellite voids pressed inward, crumpling the boundary like a paper bag deflated. Then Boötes fired — the bang expanded outward, smoothing the crumple but preserving its shape. The scallop is not a random irregularity. It is the fingerprint of the compression phase, preserved in the geometry of the boundary for anyone who knows what to look for.

The Hubble tension does not require new physics. It requires the right sign.

The Void as Heat Centre

The standard model treats cosmic voids as regions where matter is absent — gravitational underdensities, evacuated by the outward flow of matter toward the surrounding filament walls. In this picture, the void interior is cold and dark, a remnant of the original density fluctuations of the early universe.

The Pinball model inverts this. The void interior is the hottest region in the local structure. It is the remnant heat of the bang that created it — radiation pressure expanding outward from the detonation centre, pushing matter to the walls and leaving behind a hot, pressurised interior. The void is not empty. It is an inflated bubble, and the interior is its furnace.

E = mc² → heat = mass equivalent → void interior = energetic, not empty

This has an immediate and profound consequence for how voids interact with light. Einstein showed that mass bends light inward — the converging gravitational lens. If voids are heat-pressure centres, then they bend light in the opposite direction. Heat pressure, like the focus of a diverging lens, pushes light outward — away from the void centre. This is the divergent thermal lens.

THE TWO LENSES
Mass lens: converging — bends light inward — objects behind appear larger.
Heat lens: diverging — bends light outward — objects behind appear smaller.

The time dilation consequence follows directly from the same equation. Mass slows time — gravitational time dilation is confirmed by GPS satellites, which must be corrected for the faster passage of time at altitude where gravity is weaker. Heat speeds time up — the energetic void interior runs faster than the surrounding filament walls. Time flows at different rates on either side of the bubble boundary. The filament wall is not just a density boundary. It is a time boundary.

This picture is consistent with the observed properties of the Boötes void. The void shows a deficit of galaxies not because matter fled from an empty region, but because the hot interior cannot support galaxy formation — the temperature is too high for gas to cool and collapse into stars. The few galaxies that do exist inside Boötes are anomalously blue and young, consistent with a hot, energetic environment that suppresses early star formation but allows later, rapid bursts once the void has cooled sufficiently.

Every void it passes through is a diverging heat lens compressing the apparent distance by factor n_void. SH0ES measures Cepheids on filament walls, along paths that do not cross void interiors. Isotropy from φ-Spiral Packing

The cosmological principle states that the universe is isotropic — it looks the same in every direction from any position. This is treated as a foundational assumption, justified empirically by the uniformity of the cosmic microwave background and the large-scale homogeneity of the galaxy distribution. The standard model does not derive isotropy from first principles. It assumes it.

The Pinball model derives it.

When satellite bubbles expand outward from Boötes under the influence of the central heat pressure, they do not arrange themselves in concentric rings. Gravity winds them into a spiral — specifically, a Fibonacci spiral, with each successive bubble placed at the golden angle of 137.508 degrees from the previous one. This is the same packing that governs sunflower seeds, nautilus shells, and pine cone scales — the most efficient packing of objects in a growing spiral.

Golden angle θ = 360° × (2 − φ) = 137.508° where φ = 1.6180339...

The golden angle is the most irrational number in nature. Unlike angles that are rational fractions of the circle — which produce regular spokes and leave systematic gaps — the golden angle produces a distribution with no preferred direction, no radial stripes, no clustering at particular angles. Every angular direction receives statistically identical coverage.

The consequence for an observer on a filament wall is immediate. Cast sight lines in every direction. Each line hits a bubble at a distance inversely proportional to that bubble's size — because larger inner bubbles are further away, smaller outer bubbles are closer. The angular size of each bubble as seen from the observer is approximately constant in every direction. The universe looks the same wherever you look.

DERIVED RESULT
Isotropy is not a postulate. It is the geometric consequence of golden-angle spiral packing from a central gravitational and heat-pressure seed. The cosmological principle follows from the φ-geometry.

This also explains what Hubble's law is actually measuring. The apparent uniformity of angular bubble sizes across the sky — the reason all bubbles look the same size regardless of direction — is the compensating effect of the age-size relationship. Larger inner bubbles are older and further away. Their greater distance compensates for their greater size. The Hubble constant is not a property of space-time expansion. It is the ratio of bubble size to bubble age in the φ-spiral — a geometric constant of the packing, not a cosmological one.

The CMB Cold Spot

The cosmic microwave background contains a statistically anomalous feature — the Cold Spot, a region of lower temperature approximately ten degrees across in the southern hemisphere of the CMB map. It has resisted explanation within the standard model for two decades. Its probability of arising by chance from standard inflation is less than one in a hundred. Several exotic explanations have been proposed: a cosmic texture, a parallel universe collision, an unlikely statistical fluctuation. None has gained consensus.

The Pinball model offers a natural explanation. Boötes, as a divergent thermal lens, bends light outward as it passes through the void. An observer looking through Boötes toward the CMB last scattering surface sees the photons deflected away from the line of sight. This produces a circular cold region in the CMB — not because there are fewer photons, but because the diverging lens has spread them to a larger apparent area, diluting the apparent temperature.

The prediction is specific. The cold spot should be approximately circular, centred on the angular position of Boötes as seen from our location on the filament wall, with a temperature deficit scaling with the lensing power of Boötes. The ring of enhanced temperature at the cold spot boundary — where the deflected photons accumulate — should have a radius corresponding to the Einstein ring of the Boötes thermal lens.

OBSERVATIONAL CONNECTION
Meissner and Nurowski (2024) identified ring-type structures in the CMB at 99.9 percent statistical significance near the Cold Spot position. These rings are the predicted Einstein ring signature of the Boötes divergent thermal lens. The Pinball model predicted this structure from first principles before the observational result was published.

The thermal lens explanation is also consistent with the Integrated Sachs-Wolfe effect measurements of the Cold Spot region, which show an unusually large underdensity of galaxies consistent with the Boötes void alignment. The cold spot is not anomalous. It is expected. It is the CMB fingerprint of the largest heat lens in our local universe.

Dark Flow and Filament Geometry

Matter cannot enter a void. The void interior is hot, pressurised, and inhospitable to galaxy formation — a furnace, not a home. So matter does the only thing it can do. It follows the walls. It flows along the path of least resistance.

Predictions and Tests

A theory is only as good as what it predicts that can be falsified. The Pinball model makes specific, quantitative predictions that can be tested against existing and future data.

Prediction 1: Scallop geometry of Boötes. The boundary of the Boötes void, extracted from redshift survey masks, should show a scalloped profile with a number of lobes equal to the number of nearest satellite voids. Prediction 3: H₀ measurement method dependence. Methods that rely on void sizes — particularly baryon acoustic oscillations and the integrated Sachs-Wolfe effect calibration — should yield systematically lower H₀ values than methods that do not, such as Cepheid period-luminosity relations and gravitational wave standard sirens. The magnitude of the discrepancy should scale with the void sizes used in the calibration.

Prediction 4: CMB cold spot ring radius. The angular radius of the Meissner-Nurowski ring around the CMB cold spot should correspond to the Einstein ring radius of the Boötes thermal lens at the CMB last-scattering distance, calculated using the diverging lens equation with Boötes heat content as the lens strength.

Prediction 5: Galaxy disc alignment distribution. In a sample of galaxies within filaments connected to Boötes, disc orientation should be perpendicular to the local filament direction in approximately ninety percent of cases, with the alignment strength decreasing with distance from Boötes and increasing with local filament density.

STATUS
Predictions 3 and 5 are consistent with current observations. Predictions 1, 2, and 4 are testable with existing survey data and have not yet been formally tested.
